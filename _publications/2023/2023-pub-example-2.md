abstract: >-
  Narrow-based Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) offer significant diversification compared to stock market investments while maintaining a reasonable level of predictability through asset combinations and industry dependencies, rendering them ideal for the study of financial forecasting. In this research, a comparative analysis is presented focusing on Machine Learning (LightGBM), Deep Learning (LSTM), and Statistical (AR) methods for predicting the ETF market trends, with the autoregressive (AR) model outperforming across various evaluation metrics (RMSE, MAPE, R2). Beyond comparing different modelling techniques, the data aggregation approaches for constructing a multi-ticker model were discussed. The LSTM was greatly improved by the simple data concatenation, allowing complex training with a larger dataset, while the proposed shuffled-concatenation technique exhibits superior performance in LightGBM, surpassing all other models. These findings highlight the effectiveness of the multi-ticker model in improving prediction accuracy and emphasize the feasibility of forecasting shuffled time-series data using the tree-based model.
