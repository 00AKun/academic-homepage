abstract: >-
  Cover This paper proposes an adaptive collaborative con-trol framework for indoor MAV formation ffight, combining Reinforcement Learning (RL) with Model Predictive Control (MPC) – Control Barrier Function (CBF) to achieve safe and autonomous operation in constrained, dynamic environments. A Proximal Policy Optimization based agent adaptively tunes the parameters of MPC and CBF, enabling a 4 MAVs leader–follower formation to function as a self-adjusting multi-agent system. MPC provides accurate trajectory and formation tracking, while CBF enforces safety constraints during obstacle interactions. Experiments in a 15 m × 15 m × 3 m ROS-Gazebo indoor envi-ronment with dynamic obstacles demonstrate that the proposed method achieves collision-free navigation and superior perfor-mance over MPC/CBF-only baselines. Speciffcally, it attains a 100% success rate, zero collisions, and a formation error below 0.28 m, outperforming pure RL and ffxed-parameter MPC–CBF. These results show that embedding adaptive policy learning into MPC–CBF optimization enhances robustness, tracking accuracy, and cooperative behavior, representing a practical advancement toward scalable autonomous MAVs collaboration.
